USGS Topographic Maps Exercise

Activity Requirements:

- a computer or device where you can save a pdf
- free Adobe Reader installed

CAP Student Instructions:

Follow the instructions below to locate and download a geoPDF topographic map and become familar with it in Adobe Reader. Then answer the questions on the handout (also a pdf link at the end of the page).

Introduction

USGS stands for the United State Geological Survey. Visit the home page to get a glimpse of what they do.

The USGS has been creating and providing topographic maps for more than a century.

Find and Download a Topo Map

TopoView is an interactive viewer for finding, selecting and downloading topographic maps for multiple years. Some go back as far as 1879.

 

Instructions

  1. Go to the USGS topoView page.
  2. Click View and Download maps now (icon on the right) to open the topoView viewer.
  3. Find your location. Your location can be where you live or go to school or where you like to spend time. It should be a place in Connecticut that you are familiar with. Either zoom in on the map or use Search by location.
  4. Click on the map so that a list of topographic maps available at the location appears in the right panel. Notice that there are neighboring maps too. Topographic quads are delivered using a grid system so there are neighbors to the north, east, south and west.
  5. Scroll down to the most recent topographic map on the list.
  6. Click on the map name to expand the download options. Click on GeoPDF to start the download of the GeoPDF version of the topo map.
  7. Once downloaded (which may take several minutes), open the GeoPDF in Adobe Reader. This should be the program Adobe Reader and not a plug-in within a browser.
    If you are having trouble, open the Adobe Reader program, then open your saved GeoPDF.

Explore the GeoPDF Topographic Map

  1. Reduce the zoom percentage so that you can see the entire map, called a quadrangle.
  2. Notice the title at the top and text and graphics along the bottom. These items are part of the map collar.
  3. Zoom in closer to the map.
  4. Notice the different background colors, roads, brown contour lines and lots of labels. Notice the grid that runs north-south and east-west with corresponding numbers just outside the map.

Map Layers

Digital maps are made of layers. Each layer usually represents one feature type on the map (lakes or roads or structures) and usually is only points OR lines OR polygons.

One of the handy features of the GeoPDF with Adobe Reader is the ability to turn on and off different layers.

  1. Locate the Layers icon and click on it to open the Layers panel.
  2. Notice the eyeball indicating that the folder or layer is turned on (visible).
  3. Notice the > which tells you that group can be further expanded. Expand the Map Collar Group.
  4. Zoom out on your map so that the entire page is visible.
  5. Turn off Map Elements and notice what happens.
  6. Zoom in on the map and explore the layers by turning different ones on and off and expanding groups.

 

Aerial Imagery in the GeoPDF Topographic Maps

When topo maps were printed on paper and not available as digital map layers, buildings were represented on the map by small black squares. Although useful, you can imagine the work involved in keeping that buildings layer up to date on a national scale.

More recent topo maps include a layer of aerial imagery where the buildings (and many other features) are visible.

  1. Find the layer called Images and turn it on.
  2. Zoom in on the map and explore the area.

 

Questions

Now that you are familiar with a USGS Topographic map in GeoPDF format, answer the questions. Refer to the paper worksheet where there is room to write or print the pdf of the same questions.

 

EXTRA Exploration

EXTRA: Look around your map and find a stream that flows down a slope and into a river or waterbody. Usually, the water carves a ravine where it flows and the contour lines show this change in elevation. Knowing that the contour lines follow the same elevation and that water flows downhill, does the point of the V of the contour line (where it crosses the stream) point upstream or downstream?

EXTRA: Continue to explore your topographic map.

EXTRA: Download different topographic maps of this area and explore changes.

EXTRA: Download a topographic map of a different area, maybe even a different state and explore similarities and differences.

About NRCA
The Natural Resources Conservation Academy (NRCA) is an innovative program in conservation and land use planning that engages Connecticut high school students, adult conservation volunteers and teachers with conservation efforts at the community level. NRCA’s three interconnected efforts benefit students and adults by conducting education on natural resource management and geospatial technology, and facilitating the use of these new skills in the implementation of local conservation projects that benefit the community.
